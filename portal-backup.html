AGI 3D — a living intelligence you can watch form.

No score. No menus. Just a liquid-metal entity breathing, pulsing, and wobbling in real-time 3D. The camera orbits slowly. The HUD reads vital signs. Something is alive in there.

Liquid metal
Physical material with clearcoat, transmission, and metalness for a reflective wobbling surface.
Multi-layer breath
Ocean swells, pulse ripples, and chaotic micro-wobble combine into organic movement.
Ambient orbit
The camera circles the entity while dust motes and a particle ring drift through space.

It breathes

A slow global scale pulse contracts and expands the sphere like lungs filling, creating the baseline rhythm everything else layers on top of.

It pulses

Faster ripple waves race across the surface, driven by a second oscillator. The emissive glow brightens and dims with each beat.

It wobbles

Multi-octave displacement warps the geometry like a drop of liquid in zero gravity. Entropy rises and falls, making the shape drift between calm and chaotic.

Building Attract‑ion: Lessons from Subatomic Game Design

The main idea behind Attract‑ion is to understand how attraction works at the subatomic level. Electrons orbit semi-independently around the nucleus, while the nucleus itself is being affected by leptons and quarks that influence the electron's behavior in subtle but critical ways.

The goal of the game is to maintain electron stability by applying a synthetic pull force — this is you, the player, applying the pull — when the electron is about to fall out of orbit. But you can't just pull recklessly: exceed the force and the electron might collide with the nucleus, ending the run.

It's a balance act at the smallest scale imaginable. The energy fluctuations are real-time, the lepton orbitals shift unpredictably, and you have to read the field and react. Think of it as a tug-of-war where the rope is an electromagnetic bond and the stakes are hydrogen stabilization.

Building this taught me a lot about translating physics concepts into game feel. The challenge isn't simulating accuracy — it's making the player feel the tension of an electron on the edge of its orbital, one wrong tap away from collapse or collision.

Quality layer model

The goal is to make every build playable, readable, and resilient — even when it’s “just a prototype.”

Playable · Readable · Resilient
01

Clarity

Readable hierarchy, inclusive UX, and “you don’t need instructions” energy.

  • Self-guided UI with obvious next actions
  • Accessible contrast and motion-safe patterns
  • Readable typography and feedback-first states
02

Craft

Responsive layouts, performance, and a clean architecture that survives refactors.

  • Plays cleanly on mobile → ultrawide
  • Lightweight assets and predictable frame time
  • Modular components with clear ownership
03

Trust

Secure defaults, privacy awareness, and resilient engineering habits.

  • Least-privilege inputs and safe defaults
  • Privacy-first data collection
  • Clear error handling and recovery paths
04

Measurement

Clear events and outcomes so agents (and humans) can iterate with confidence.

  • Human-auditable events and outcomes
  • Easy experiments with rollback paths
  • Iteration-ready metrics for every build
