FAQ

How do we stabilize the orbit?
Scroll up to pull the electron inward and scroll down to push it outward. Make small corrections and keep the orbit inside the band for the full hold time. Band width: ±— px (≈—% of target radius).
How strong is a pulse?
Pulses are disabled. Use the mouse wheel instead: wheel up applies pull-in and wheel down applies push-out in — field steps.

Knowledge base

Leptons

Electrons are leptons: light particles that do not feel the strong force.

  • Carry negative electric charge (e⁻).
  • Orbit behavior is modeled here as a toy 2D system.

Quarks

Quarks combine to form hadrons such as protons and neutrons.

  • Proton = two up quarks + one down quark.
  • Bound by the strong interaction.

Hadron colliders

Large accelerators collide hadrons to probe fundamental interactions.

  • Collisions reveal particle behavior at high energies.
  • Data informs models used in particle physics.
