# Atomic Identity & Structure — Sodium (Na)

## Identity
- **Element name:** Sodium
- **Symbol:** Na
- **Atomic number (Z):** 11
  - Defines sodium uniquely: **11 protons** in the nucleus.
- **Standard atomic weight:** ~22.98976928 u (often rounded to **22.99 u**)

## Nucleus
- **Protons:** 11 (fixed for sodium)
- **Neutrons:** varies by isotope
  - Most abundant stable isotope is **sodium-23**: 11 protons + **12 neutrons**

### Isotopes (overview)
- **Stable:** \(^{23}\text{Na}\) (dominant in nature)
- **Radioisotopes:** many exist (e.g., \(^{22}\text{Na}\), \(^{24}\text{Na}\)); they differ only by neutron count and nuclear stability.

## Electrons
A neutral sodium atom has **11 electrons**.

### Ground-state electron configuration
- **Spectroscopic notation:** 1s² 2s² 2p⁶ 3s¹
- **Noble-gas shorthand:** [Ne] 3s¹

### Shell (principal energy level) distribution (Bohr-style)
- **K shell (n=1):** 2 electrons
- **L shell (n=2):** 8 electrons
- **M shell (n=3):** 1 electron

So the simple shell count is: **2–8–1**.

### Valence electron
- **Valence electrons:** 1 (the 3s¹ electron)
- This single outer electron is the key structural feature behind sodium’s placement and many of its atomic trends.

## Quantum numbers (outermost electron, ground state)
For the 3s¹ electron (ground state):
- Principal quantum number: \(n = 3\)
- Orbital angular momentum: \(\ell = 0\) (s orbital)
- Magnetic quantum number: \(m_\ell = 0\)
- Spin: \(m_s = \pm 1/2\)

## Atomic mass vs atomic number (clarifier)
- **Atomic number (11):** counts protons; never changes for sodium.
- **Mass number (A):** protons + neutrons (e.g., 23 for \(^{23}\text{Na}\)).
- **Atomic weight (~22.99 u):** abundance-weighted average mass of natural sodium atoms.
